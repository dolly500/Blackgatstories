About Us

Our Mission:

At Blackgatstories, we are on a mission to empower the Black community by sharing authentic stories that celebrate the richness and diversity of Black lives. We believe that every story matters and that being Black is an honor worth sharing with the world. Our company was born out of a deep desire to reshape the narrative around Black stories, breaking free from stereotypes and misconceptions.

Why Support Us:

By supporting Blackgatstories, you're not just supporting a storytelling company; you're endorsing a movement that seeks to amplify the Black voice and inspire positive change. We're here to challenge stereotypes, pass down wisdom, and inspire lives, all while promoting diversity, inclusion, and equality. Your support is instrumental in reshaping the world's perception of Black stories.

What We Do

Our Diverse Services includes:

Podcasts

Join us on a journey to discover and celebrate Black entrepreneurs and their incredible stories of success. Our podcast aims to uncover the secrets behind building a global brand and becoming an expert in your field.

PSA (Public Service Announcement)

Stay informed and inspired through our innovative PSA platform, where we deliver real-time stories and news from around the world, both the challenges and the triumphs.

Documentaries

Delve into our culturally rich documentaries, where we explore the multifaceted aspects of Black culture, unveiling stories that have been underrepresented or untold. These documentaries also explore our spiritual and emotional beliefs.

BGS Vox Pop

Get a global perspective on issues, ideas, and culture from Black communities around the world, particularly those with unique fan bases on social media

OUR NOMINEES

Abraham Kahasha Kabral

Cultural Heritage Guardian

×

Abraham Kahasha Kabral is a Congolese peacebuilder, climate and migration activist, social change activist and Founder and Executive Director of The World and Peoples in Transformation (WOPT) where he focuses his work on the positive transformation of world conflicts, facilitating dialogue as a mediator, accepting discussions, disagreements and investing in trust between warring parties and to put them back on a common solution.  He studied in the DRC, Burundi, Kenya and Egypt at prestigious universities in various fields of leadership linked to positive peace, people's governance and climate change. Abraham KABRAL is also the founder and chairman of HAM Groupe (HG) , an international investement group of companies where Abraham is committed to transform africa , creating jobs , improving african Economy trought private sectors.

Dotun Popoola

Afro artistry luminary

×

Artist Bio Dotun Popoola is a synergetic metal sculptor who has carved a niche for himself with his polychromatic scrap metal works. Born in Agege, Lagos, in April 1981, Dotun had his primary and secondary education in Agege, Lagos. He had his first and second degrees in Fine and Applied Arts with specialisations in sculpture and painting, respectively, at Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife, Nigeria. Dotun trained in several art studios before and after his university education. He was certified as a hybrid metal sculptor at John Lopez Studio in South Dacota, USA, in 2015. Dotun has won several awards, including the Director General’s Award for the Best Artist of the Year in the 2009 NYSC Arts Competition, which was the maiden edition. Dotun was featured in 2018 at the West Africa International Art Fair, “ARTX Lagos.” He represented Nigeria at the maiden scrap art exhibition in Souq Waqif, Qatar, in 2019 and at the Global Art Festival in Gujarat, India, in 2020. Additionally, he participated in Coexistence in Turkey in 2021 and the valuable scraps exhibition in Turkey in 2022. His hybrid sculpture took centre stage at the Nigerian pavilion at Expo 2020 in Dubai, leaving world leaders speechless. Dotun has participated in thirty (30) group, fourteen (14) joint, and six (6) solo exhibitions. He is passionate about touching lives through his artistic and leadership skills, turning trash into treasures and rubbish into rubies.
